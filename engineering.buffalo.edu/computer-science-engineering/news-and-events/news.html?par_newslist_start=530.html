  • New training program will make advanced manufacturing more secure
    9/26/22

    A National Science Foundation-funded project led by faculty members in the University at Buffalo’s School of Engineering and Applied Sciences will provide critical training on Cyber-Infrastructure technologies for the next generation of research in the advanced manufacturing workforce. 

  • UB joins forces with IIT Jodhpur to create a joint PhD program in computer science and engineering
    9/22/22
    The United States and India have long been leaders in the education and development of engineers. Now, a new joint PhD program in computer science and engineering between the University at Buffalo and the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Jodhpur will strengthen this international partnership.
  • A cheating feud involving world champion Magnus Carlsen and a 19-year-old prodigy is rocking the chess world
    9/20/22
    Insider interviewed Kenneth Regan, associate professor of computer science and engineering, about cheating accusations casted by world champion Magnus Carlsen against Hans Niemann.
  • 7 Best Places to Learn Blockchain Technology Online for FREE
    9/19/22
    A Medium article on the best places to learn blockchain technology for free recommends an online course offered by UB on Coursera.
  • UB ranked among top national universities in U.S. News & World Report
    9/12/22

    UB is No. 38 among public universities, according to the rankings.

  • Nine undergraduates work with UB Faculty as part of 2022 LSAMP summer program
    9/12/22

    For the first time in three years, students and faculty members were able to come together, and work in-person, on some of society’s most pressing issues as part of the summer LSAMP research program. 

  • Chess is in chaos over suspicion that a player cheated against Magnus Carlsen
    9/8/22
    The Wall Street Journal quoted Kenneth Regan, professor of computer science and engineering, in an article on the chaos that ensued when world champion chess player Magnus Carlsen withdrew from a major tournament after a stunning loss to opponent Hans Moke Niemann, igniting suspicions of foul play by Niemann.
  • UB engineering researchers receive NSF CAREER awards totaling $2.3 million
    9/7/22

    Their projects will tackle pressing societal concerns ranging from racial inequality in the U.S. to PFAS pollution, the need for better semiconductors, and energy conservation.

  • A day in LA with the deepfake artists trying to make the digital world as real as the physical one
    9/1/22
    Dot.LA reported on the widespread use of deepfake content online and quoted Siwei Lyu, SUNY Empire Innovation Professor in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, director of University at Buffalo’s Media Forensic Lab and the founding co-director of the university’s Center for Information Integrity.
  • Art, engineering team up to tackle oyster issues for new exhibit
    8/22/22
    Can an oyster listen? What kind of sound environment is needed for oysters to thrive? Those are the kinds of questions UB faculty member Stephanie Rothenberg is exploring in a new art exhibit with help from a few computer science and engineering students.
