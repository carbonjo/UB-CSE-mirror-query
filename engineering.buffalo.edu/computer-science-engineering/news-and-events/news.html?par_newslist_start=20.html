News

  • AI-altered clip features fabricated Modi comments on Afghanistan-Pakistan conflict
    11/10/25

    Agence-France Presse Pakistan utilized the DeepFake-o-Meter, developed by Siwei Lyu and his team in the Media Forensic Lab, to prove that a video showing the Indian Prime Minister commenting on the Afghanistan-Pakistan conflict was digitally altered. 

  • How FIDE tries to ensure there is no cheating at a prestigious event like the Chess World Cup
    11/8/25

    The Indian Express reported that Ken Regan is responsible for the creation of a software program that the International Chess Federation uses to detect cheating devices ahead of the Chess World Cup. 

  • No, COAS Upendra Dwivedi Didn’t Say Army Will Reduce Non-Hindu Soldiers
    11/8/25

    RNA news utilized the DeepFake-O-Meter, developed by Siwei Lyu and his team in the Media Forensic Lab, to discredit AI-generated voiceovers of an Indian military general and another military official.

  • Power 100 Leaders of Color: Meet the top 10 for 2025
    11/7/25

    Buffalo Business First published the top 10 in its Power 100 Leaders of Color list, which includes UB President Satish K. Tripathi.

  • LC Singh’s Book The Collapse of Illusions Launched; Introduces a New Dimension in How We Understand Emotions
    11/5/25

    India Education Diary quoted UB President Satish K. Tripathi in an article about a new book by LC Singh called “The Collapse of Illusions.”

  • Rohini Srihari discusses chatbots on UB's Driven to Discover podcast
    11/4/25

    Rohini Srihari, professor of computer science and engineering, was a guest on UB's Driven to Discover podcast, where she discussed how to make chatbots better.

  • Rohini Srihari on Making Chatbots Better
    11/4/25
    An AI pioneer who combines scientific expertise with a love for language, Rohini Srihari is the ideal person to reimagine chatbots. In this episode, she talks about building conversational AI tools that create positive change, from tackling the nation’s mental health crisis to helping people with motor neuron diseases find their voice.
  • Ed Tech Can Unlock STEM Potential of Students With Disabilities — If It’s Funded
    11/2/25

    An opinion piece in The 74 mentioned the work of UB’s National AI Institute for Exceptional Education, directed by Venu Govindaraju, in developing AI tools to help children with speech and language challenges.

  • How Sora is outsmarting human deepfake detection
    10/28/25

    Fast Company quoted Siwei Lyu in an article about how Open AI’s new video generation software is creating deepfakes that are outsmarting current detection programs. 

  • A young chess star’s death, cheating accusations and a sport in turmoil
    10/27/25

    The Washington Post quoted Ken Regan in an article about Daniel Naroditsky, a young chess grandmaster who passed away last month.
